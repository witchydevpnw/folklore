# Shelf Life — Launch Copy

Three pieces for the June 1, 2026 Shelf Life kick-off. Written to send as-is or edit before use. Placeholders in `[brackets]` are context-specific details to fill in at send time.

## 1. Email to free library and book box stewards (when they reach out)

**Use when:** someone contacts Folklore after seeing the Facebook group post, a flyer in their book box, or the social kick-off post. This is a warm reply to an inbound inquiry, not a cold outreach.

**Subject:** Shelf Life — thanks for reaching out

Hi [Name],

Thanks for getting in touch about Shelf Life. Here's the short version of how it works and what we'd ask of you.

Every quarter, we publish a shortlist of 3 books by local and regional authors. Readers in the Tri-Cities vote on their favorite (each vote is a $5 donation to Folklore Society). The winning book gets purchased from a local indie bookstore, and we place copies in free libraries and book exchanges across the metro. Every copy has a small Folklore bookplate inside with the book's title, where it was purchased, and the quarter it went out.

We're building our first roster of partner locations now, aiming for geographic spread across Richland, West Richland, Kennewick, and Pasco. Your [library/book box] at [address or general area] would be a great fit.

What we'd ask of you:

- Place the book in your library when it arrives (we'll deliver or mail it to you with a short note).
- Let us know when the book leaves. A text or DM is plenty.
- If you're willing, snap a photo of the book on the shelf. Steward photos are the best content we'll have for our quarterly reports and social media.

No cost to you, ever. No commitment beyond one quarter at a time. If it's not working, no hard feelings. If you want to stay on, we'll keep you in the rotation.

We're kicking off the first cycle this summer. If you're in, just reply with your book exchange's street address and any logistical notes we should know (size of the box, seasonal closures, anything like that). If you have a Little Free Library charter number, include that too, but it's not required. Registered LFLs and homegrown book boxes are equally welcome.

Questions? Ask away.

Warmly,
Candace Delany
President, Folklore Society
hello@folkloresocietywa.org
folkloresocietywa.org

## 2. Email to local bookstores

### Version A: New-book stores (BAGG, Wildflower Fiction, Ink Drinkers, Adventures Underground)

**Use when:** reaching out to the indie bookstores you already know and frequent. The tone here is neighbor-to-neighbor, not cold outreach. You're formalizing a relationship that already exists in some form.

**Subject:** Shelf Life — a new Folklore program that involves you

Hi [Name],

This is Candace from Folklore Society. I'm a regular at [store name] and I've been watching the indie bookstore scene here grow into something real. That's part of why we started Folklore.

We're launching a quarterly program called Shelf Life, and I wanted to loop you in because your store is part of how it works.

The short version: every quarter, we publish a shortlist of 3 books by local and regional authors. Readers vote on their favorite (each vote is a $5 donation to Folklore). The winning book gets purchased from whichever Tri-Cities indie bookstore carries it, and copies go into free libraries and book exchanges across the metro with a Folklore bookplate inside naming the store where they were purchased.

We always buy at full retail. The orders will be small (5-15 copies per quarter), and we want to be an easy, repeat customer, not someone asking for favors.

I know your store pretty well, but for our program records I'd love to get a few things on paper:

1. What genres do you typically stock for newer releases by regional or indie authors?
2. Can you special-order a title outside your usual stock if we need it? If so, what's the typical turnaround?
3. What's the easiest way for us to place an order? Walk in, email, call?
4. Are you comfortable being named in our materials and quarterly reports as one of the bookstores we buy from?

Our first cycle kicks off June 1 and we're theming it around Pride month. I'd love to have you on board from the start.

Happy to grab coffee or talk through any of this in person. You can always reach me at hello@folkloresocietywa.org.

Thanks for the work you do here.

Candace Delany
President, Folklore Society
folkloresocietywa.org

### Version B: Used and specialty bookstores (A Lettered Home, Xenophile Bibliophile, Storytime Bookshop)

**Use when:** reaching out to stores that primarily sell used, collectible, or specialty books. Shelf Life requires purchasing new copies, so these stores aren't a natural fit for this specific program. But the relationship matters, and there may be a place for them in future programming.

**Subject:** Quick hello from Folklore Society

Hi [Name],

This is Candace from Folklore Society. I'm a customer and a fan of what you've built at [store name].

We're a literary nonprofit based here in the Tri-Cities, and we're starting to build out programs that connect readers, authors, and the indie bookstores in the area. Our first program, Shelf Life, involves purchasing new copies of books by local authors and placing them in free libraries across the metro. Because it requires ordering new titles, it's centered on stores that stock or can order new releases.

That said, I didn't want you to hear about the program secondhand and wonder why you weren't included. You're part of this community and we know it. We're thinking about future programming where used and specialty bookstores are a natural fit, and I'd love to keep the conversation open for when that takes shape.

In the meantime, if you ever start stocking or ordering new titles by regional authors, we'd love to add you to the Shelf Life roster. And if there's anything else Folklore can do to support what you're building, I'm all ears.

Reach me anytime at hello@folkloresocietywa.org.

Candace Delany
President, Folklore Society
folkloresocietywa.org

## 3. Social kick-off post (June 1)

**Platform:** Instagram and Facebook feed post. Can be adapted for story format by trimming to the first two paragraphs plus the CTA.

**Post copy:**

We've been thinking about what it means to have a reading life closer to home. Not just the events and the book clubs and the late nights doing bookish trivia (though we love all of that). But the quieter thing. A book you didn't expect, sitting in a free library on your street, placed there because someone in your city voted for it.

📚 That's Shelf Life.
Every quarter, we put up a shortlist of three books by local and regional authors. You vote for your favorite. We buy copies of the winning book from a Tri-Cities indie bookstore and place them in free libraries and book exchanges across the metro, with a Folklore bookplate inside.
We're launching during Pride month, and our first shortlist celebrates that 🏳️‍🌈
We need three things to make this work: authors willing to put their books forward, readers who want to nominate something great, and people who maintain free libraries or book boxes who'd let us add a book to their shelf. If any of that sounds like you, the links are in our bio.
This one is going to be fun 🌙
