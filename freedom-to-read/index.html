What's happening✦

Three local crews, one stubborn idea: you should get to read whatever you want. Obsidian Book Collective dreamed it up, BAGG Books is throwing open its doors, and Folklore Society is pulling the taps — and we want the whole Tri-Cities in on it. One afternoon, free and open to everyone, for every book somebody tried to ban.

  • Banned romance, front and center — the good stuff, pulled right off BAGG's shelves and proudly on display
  • A free book swap — bring a stack you're done with, leave with a new-to-you pile
  • Bookish crafts & temporary tattoos — wear your reading on your sleeve
  • A beer garden out back — music, yard games, and plenty of room to linger

Folklore on the bar✦

Pull up a chair — the beer garden is Folklore's corner of the day. Every pour goes straight back into our general fund, which keeps the lights on across all our programs. Buy a round, fund a reading.

Want your gift to go somewhere specific? Our Author Fund is raising money right now to bring real, working writers to the Tri-Cities. Cash gifts are always welcome, and every dollar adds up.

Who's behind this✦

It takes a village — or in this case, three of them. A bookshop, a book collective, and a literary society pooling space, shelves, and time to pull off something none of us could do alone.
