Coming fall 2026

Round Table

When your local book club is reading a regional author's book, Folklore buys the copies for the group. Vendor-direct, from a Tri-Cities indie bookstore of your choice.

Opening fall 2026

Round Table is in design now. We're using 2026 to confirm bookstore partnerships and finalize the request form, with the first cohort of book clubs launching this fall. Sign up for the newsletter below and we'll send the heads-up when requests open.

How it works

01

Your book club asks.

You're reading a book by a regional author. Tell us about your club and the book you're reading. We'll ask which Tri-Cities indie bookstore you'd like us to use.

02

We confirm eligibility.

A mechanical check against published criteria. We're confirming your club is open and meets regularly. Once approved, we contact the bookstore.

03

Folklore pays the bookstore.

Vendor-direct payment. Funds never touch the book club or its members. We pay full retail, as we always do.

04

You read together.

The bookstore has your copies ready for pickup. A short note after your meeting helps us tell Round Table's story to donors.

What we look for

Your book club

  • Local to the Tri-Cities (member residency, not where you meet)
  • Open membership: anyone can join
  • Free: no membership dues or paid access
  • Non-exclusive: not gender-restricted, religion-restricted, or otherwise gatekept to a specific group
  • Real and recurring: meets on a regular schedule, not a one-off event

The book

  • Author based in the Pacific Northwest (Tri-Cities and Eastern Washington prioritized)
  • Published in the last five years (no manuscripts or preorders)
  • Available through a Tri-Cities indie bookstore (your choice of which one)
  • Written for young adult or adult readers

Year 1 limits

Year 1 is a pilot. We're supporting up to four Round Tables, with up to 12 copies per request and one Round Table per book club per calendar year. Caps grow as the program proves out and as the fundraising track record grows with it.

If demand exceeds capacity in any given month, requests are evaluated in order received. We'll keep a waitlist if needed.

Common questions

What counts as "local"?

For book clubs: members live in the Tri-Cities (Richland, West Richland, Kennewick, Pasco). For authors: based in the Pacific Northwest, with priority for Tri-Cities and Eastern Washington writers.

What does "open membership" mean?

A new reader who hears about your group should be able to show up to a meeting. Clubs that limit who can join based on gender, religion, or similar identity criteria aren't a fit for Round Table.

Can my book club pick the bookstore?

Yes. As long as it's a Tri-Cities indie bookstore that can order the title, you choose where the books come from. Different stores carry different genres, so the right answer often depends on what your club is reading.

Are there content restrictions?

Yes, the same as Shelf Life. Books whose primary purpose is religious instruction or evangelism are not eligible (fiction and nonfiction that engage with religion as a theme are welcome). Books that promote discrimination based on race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability, or national origin are not eligible. Folklore also reserves the right to exclude any title whose author is the subject of credible public allegations of abuse, harassment, or predatory conduct.

Why only four Round Tables in Year 1?

Round Table is a pilot in its first year. Four is enough to learn how the program works end to end without overcommitting Folklore's resources. As donor support and operational rhythm grow, so does the cap. The point is to start small and build from there.

What if my book club has more than 12 members?

For Year 1, we cap purchases at 12 copies per request. Larger clubs are absolutely welcome to apply. We'd buy 12 copies and trust the group to share or rotate them. As funding grows, so will the cap.
