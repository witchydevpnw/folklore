# General Fund — Setup & Copy Pack

Folklore Society's unrestricted giving option. Covers the operating costs that keep all four programs running: bar supplies, venue rentals, insurance, licensing, software, print costs, and general overhead.

This isn't a named program like Author Fund or Shelf Life. It's a fourth option on the donation page for donors who want Folklore to deploy funds flexibly.

---

## What the General Fund covers

An honest breakdown so donors know what they're supporting:

- **Event operations.** Bar inventory (beer, wine, spirits, cups, ice, barware), Special Occasion License fees ($90 per event), venue rentals when not partner-covered, event signage and print materials.
- **Compliance and licensing.** State annual report ($10), charity registration renewal ($60), WA business license, Fan Zone license if pursued ($185/day).
- **Insurance.** General liability, D&O, liquor liability (~$730-1,750/year combined).
- **Infrastructure.** Domain and hosting, Zeffy and Square operational costs, Canva (while awaiting 501(c)(3)), bookkeeping software, email.
- **Print and physical materials.** Shelf Life bookplates, Dedication labels, event signage, printed programs.
- **Flexible reserve.** Opportunities that don't fit the other programs: a new book club wanting underwriting, or a last-minute sponsorship of a regional literary event.

## Naming

### Recommendation: don't name it

Author Fund, Shelf Life, Dedication, and Signature are named because each does something specific. A general fund is by definition not specific. Giving it a proper name adds something to explain without adding something to support.

The cleaner move: make the fourth donation option a functional CTA, not a branded program.

**Lead language: "Give where it's needed most."** Warm, inviting, action-oriented, makes the flexibility clear.

If you later want a named version, second choice would be **The Anthology** (metaphor of many small contributions combining into something whole). Hold in reserve and launch with the functional label first.

## Placement on folkloresocietywa.org

On the donation page, four giving options in this order (from mission-specific to mission-flexible):

1. **Author Fund** — Bring writers to the Tri-Cities
2. **Shelf Life** — Put books in Little Free Libraries
3. **Dedication** — Sponsor a bottle at a Folklore event
4. **Give where it's needed most** — Support general operations

The order matters. Named programs first, flexible option last. This lets mission-motivated donors find the program that resonates, and leaves the functional option for donors who've already decided to give and just need the easiest path.
