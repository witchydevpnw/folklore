# Author Fund — Reference Summary

Folklore Society's central fundraising vehicle. Underwrites author visits to the Tri-Cities: honoraria, travel, lodging, meals, and related hosting costs.

This is a working reference summary. The formal governance documents are the **Author Fund Framework** (fundraising structure, tiers, reporting) and the **Author Engagement Policy** (how events are funded and executed), both already on file alongside the **Author Engagement Agreement Template** (author-side legal doc).

---

## What Author Fund is

A restricted giving category within Folklore's donation platform. Donors contribute with the understanding that their gift supports the hosting of visiting authors at Folklore-sponsored events in the Tri-Cities. Events are produced in partnership with BAGG Books, Obsidian Book Collective, and other regional literary partners. Folklore's role is to fundraise, underwrite, and sponsor. Partner entities handle event production.

## Tier structure (locked)

Each tier is tied to an actual cost of hosting a visiting author. The framing is concrete because the numbers are real:

- **$25:** A warm welcome for a visiting author (per diem incidentals)
- **$50:** Meals for a visiting author (lunch and dinner)
- **$150:** A night of lodging (matches GSA per diem for Tri-Cities)
- **$500:** Bring a debut indie author out to read
- **$1,500:** Fully sponsor a regional author event

The "every contribution adds up" framing is core to how the tiers are pitched. A $25 gift from 30 people is $750, which exceeds a full tier of real cost. Copy should reinforce this whenever possible.

## Recognition tiers (Framework, Section 4)

Donor recognition applies across all Folklore giving, not just Author Fund. Opt-in is required for any public recognition.

- **Folklore Friend** ($25–$99): Listed in quarterly Author Fund Report with consent
- **Folklore Reader** ($100–$249): Above, plus named in next event program, social thank-you
- **Folklore Patron** ($250–$499): Above, plus featured thank-you in newsletter
- **Folklore Sponsor** ($500–$999): Above, plus sponsor block in event program, event signage recognition
- **Folklore Benefactor** ($1,000–$2,499): Above, plus signed book from an upcoming author (subject to author consent)
- **Presenting Sponsor** ($2,500+): Above, plus named presenting sponsor status on event promotional materials and at the event itself

## Fund structure (Framework, Section 2)

- **General Author Fund (unrestricted):** default category; Board allocates across author engagements as needs and opportunities arise; contributions carry forward year-over-year
- **Designated Contributions (restricted):** tied to a specific author tier or engagement; must be used for the designated purpose within 12 months or reallocated with donor acknowledgement (or refunded)
- All Author Fund gifts deposited to the Corporation's operating account, tracked by category in Wave

## Reporting commitments (Framework, Section 6)

- **Quarterly Author Fund Report** published at folkloresocietywa.org: contributions received, events funded, current balance, upcoming commitments, recognized donors (with consent)
- **Live progress indicator** on website, updated no less than monthly
- **Annual Author Fund Summary** at end of fiscal year, suitable for grant applications and donor communications

## Operational platforms

- **Donation processing:** Zeffy (0% platform fee)
- **Banking:** Crowded (pre-501(c)(3) bridge; moves to traditional banking post-determination)
- **Accounting:** Wave, with Author Fund contributions tracked by category
- **Website:** progress indicator and quarterly reports hosted at folkloresocietywa.org

## Launch phases (Framework, Section 10)

- **Phase 1 (current, pre-501(c)(3)):** Accept contributions as unrestricted gifts; build donor list
- **Phase 2 (post-501(c)(3)):** Update deductibility language; send retroactive acknowledgement letters to Phase 1 donors
- **Phase 3 (first funded event):** Launch a campaign-style push tied to a specific confirmed author
- **Phase 4 (recurring giving):** Introduce monthly giving as a Sustaining Member tier
- **Phase 5 (grant eligibility):** Pursue literary and community grants using documented track record

## How Author Fund relates to other programs

- **Shelf Life:** separate restricted campaign; different ask, different use of funds. Both have donor recognition through the same tier structure.
- **Dedication:** $25 sponsored-bottle program tied to bar events. Operationally adjacent to Author Fund (both involve the bar operation), but a distinct donor ask. A Dedication donor isn't the same gift as an Author Fund donor at $25 — they're choosing the emotional experience, not the cost bucket.
- **Signature:** launches 2027. Separate restricted campaign for publishing grants to indie authors.
- **General Fund:** unrestricted giving for operational overhead. Fourth donation-page option behind the three named programs.
