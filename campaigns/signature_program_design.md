# Signature — Program Design Document

Signature is Folklore Society's publishing grant program for indie authors. It's designed to launch in early 2027 (fiscal Year 2), after the Author Fund and Shelf Life have established a track record.

This document captures everything decided about the program so far, flags what's still open, and outlines the 2026 work plan needed to reach launch.

---

## What Signature is

A grant program that helps indie authors cover specific publishing costs. "Indie" here means not picked up by a major publishing house. Small press, local publisher, and self-published authors are all eligible.

Grants are vendor-direct: Folklore pays the editor, cover designer, printer, or other vendor named by the selected author. Folklore never hands cash to the author. This keeps the program structurally clean under IRS private-benefit rules and gives Folklore real working relationships with the regional publishing ecosystem.

## Locked decisions

- **Name:** Signature
- **Launch:** Early 2027 (fiscal Year 2)
- **Cadence:** Annual
- **Scale Year 1:** 3 grants (pilot; scales with fundraising track record)
- **Grant ceiling:** $500 per grant
- **Payment structure:** Vendor-direct
- **Application style:** Flexible. Author describes their project and names the vendor the grant would go to; the panel evaluates.
- **Selection:** Panel-based, no board members as voting members
- **Eligibility:** Indie author, geographic priority Tri-Cities → Eastern WA → PNW
- **Board oversight:** Via Framework governance (Section 4 approval authority, Section 12 amendments)

## Still to be designed in 2026

### Panel
- Composition (suggested: 3 or 5 members; a working writer, a serious reader, and someone with editing or publishing background is a reasonable mix)
- Recruitment (who, from where)
- Conflict of interest process (disclosure, recusal procedures)
- Terms of service (one cycle, two cycles, rotating seats)

### Selection criteria (published in advance)

Starting sketch, to be refined:

1. Author's connection to the region
2. Project stage and readiness
3. Clarity about how the $500 would be used and to which vendor
4. Potential reach or artistic merit of the project
5. Whether $500 would meaningfully advance the project

### Application mechanics
- Application form (platform: Google Forms, Zeffy, custom?)
- Required fields (name, project description, vendor, budget, bio, sample if applicable)
- Application window dates
- Scoring rubric
- How applications are distributed to the panel

### Vendor payment process
- How Folklore cuts checks (Crowded account mechanics)
- Vendor W-9 requirement
- Invoice submission process
- Timeline from grant award to vendor payment

### Author agreement
- Short one-pager confirming grant terms, vendor relationship, and public recognition
- Clarification that Folklore retains no rights in the resulting book
- Attribution expectations (does the book need to acknowledge Folklore? Recommended, not required)

### Donor and website copy
- Zeffy campaign (likely a second option on Author Fund page in 2026, separate page for 2027)
- "About Signature" page on folkloresocietywa.org
- Quarterly report inclusion once launched

## 2026 work plan

The advantage of a Year 2 launch is a long runway. Work can happen in the margins of the main 2026 programs.

### Spring–Summer 2026 (through September)
- Start informal outreach to potential panel members
- Begin building relationships with regional editors, cover designers, and printers
- Draft the full selection criteria and scoring rubric

### Fall 2026 (October–December)
- Finalize panel roster
- Draft and attorney-review the author agreement
- Build the application form
- Draft and stage donor copy for soft launch
- Attorney sign-off on private-benefit compliance

### January 2027
- Applications open
- Parallel: Signature donor campaign goes live on website and Zeffy

### February 2027
- Applications close
- Panel begins review

### March 2027
- Grants announced
- Vendor payment process begins as authors name vendors and vendors submit invoices

## Attorney flag

Before applications open in early 2027, confirm with attorney that Signature as designed satisfies IRS private-benefit rules for 501(c)(3) grants to individuals. The structure is built to pass muster (published objective criteria, panel-based selection, vendor-direct payments), but formal sign-off protects Folklore and makes the program defensible in any future audit. Flag on the attorney's list in fall 2026 so it's not a scramble at the end.
