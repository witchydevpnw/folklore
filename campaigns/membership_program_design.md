# Membership — Program Design Document

Membership is Folklore Society's planned community-model program, designed to turn regular supporters into a recognized in-group at Folklore events. It's deferred to early 2027 (fiscal Year 2), with design work happening in fall 2026 alongside the Signature program.

This document captures the direction chosen, why the deferral makes sense, and what needs to be decided during the fall 2026 design window.

---

## What Membership is

A community-model membership program. Dues are secondary; the membership confers tangible belonging at Folklore events. The anchor perk is an etched glass or cup that members bring to events (or that Folklore stores and sets out for them). Around that anchor, members get early access to ticketed events, an annual member-only gathering, and whatever other perks emerge from Year 1 event patterns.

This is distinct from the four giving programs (Author Fund, Shelf Life, Dedication, Signature) and from the General Fund. Those are ways to give to specific missions. Membership is a way to belong to the organization.

## Why deferred to 2027

Three reasons the 2026 launch would have been premature:

1. **Event pattern not yet established.** Perks like "early ticket access" only work if events have real demand. After a year of programming, Folklore will know whether events sell out, which kinds of events draw the biggest crowds, and what "member moment" at an event could actually look like.

2. **Upfront cost of physical perks.** Etched glassware is a real expense. Ordering 50 cups before knowing whether 50 people will sustain the program is a bet better placed after Year 1.

3. **Pre-qualified member list from recurring donors.** The Author Fund Framework (Section 10, Phase 4) already contemplates a "Sustaining Member" recognition for recurring monthly donors. By the end of 2026, the recurring donor base becomes the natural pre-qualified list for membership launch. Launching membership too early would create a parallel list before there's a population to draw from.

## Locked decisions

- **Model:** Community-model (belonging over revenue)
- **Launch:** Early 2027 (fiscal Year 2)
- **Design window:** Fall 2026, in parallel with Signature
- **2026 posture:** No formal membership program. If asked, the answer is "we're building toward that; recurring monthly giving gets you on the list for when it launches."
- **Anchor perk:** Etched glassware for member use at Folklore events
- **Additional perks (confirmed direction):** early access to tickets and events, annual member-only gathering

## Still to be designed in fall 2026

### Dues structure
- Annual vs monthly cadence
- Price point (Year 1 budget assumed $25-50/year; this is an anchor, not a commitment)
- Whether monthly recurring donors at other programs auto-qualify for membership at a threshold
- Tax treatment if any perks exceed nominal value (likely not, but worth confirming)

### Glassware specifics
- Glass vs cup vs both (different events, different vessels)
- Etched with what (Folklore wordmark, member's name, a number, a combination)
- Replacement policy (one per year, paid replacements, break-and-replace-free)
- Storage model (members bring their own, Folklore stores them at partner venues, hybrid)
- Minimum order quantity and unit cost (drives the price floor for dues)

### Early access mechanics
- How much earlier (24 hours, 48 hours, a full week)
- Which events qualify (all ticketed, or just high-demand ones)
- How it's enforced operationally on Zeffy or the ticketing platform

### Annual member event
- Format (salon, reading circle, pre-show reception, dinner)
- Timing (Q1 to reset the year, or around an existing high-energy event)
- Cost per attendee and how it's covered by dues
- Author involvement if any (needs to fit within Author Engagement Policy)

### Recognition alignment
- How membership sits next to the Framework's existing tiers (Folklore Friend through Presenting Sponsor)
- Whether Sustaining Members get a visual mark in the quarterly report
- Whether membership counts toward cumulative giving recognition

### Platform and operations
- Zeffy campaign vs separate membership platform
- Member list management (who tracks it, where it lives, how renewals are handled)
- Annual renewal mechanics and lapsed-member policy
- Member-facing communications cadence (newsletter, updates, invitations)

## What 2026 does for the 2027 launch

The year isn't empty. Several natural outputs of 2026 feed directly into the membership design:

- **Recurring donor list.** Author Fund Framework Phase 4 introduces monthly giving in 2026. That list is the pre-qualified membership base.
- **Event pattern data.** Number of events, attendance, sellout rate, repeat attendees. Tells you whether early access is a meaningful perk.
- **Venue relationships.** By end of 2026, Folklore will have running relationships with BAGG Books and other venues. Storage of member glassware (if chosen) becomes a logistical conversation with known partners, not a cold ask.
- **Donor recognition tier validation.** Running the Framework's recognition tiers for a year surfaces what works and what doesn't. Membership recognition is designed on top of those learnings rather than in parallel.

## 2026-to-2027 timeline

### Spring-Summer 2026
- No membership work. Focus stays on Author Fund, Shelf Life, Dedication, and first-event execution.
- Track recurring donor signups as they come in.

### Fall 2026 (October-December)
- Begin membership design in parallel with Signature.
- Draft dues structure, perks package, and operational workflow.
- Source glassware vendors and price samples.
- Draft Zeffy campaign and website copy.
- Attorney review of any provisions that touch tax treatment or donor-recognition rules.

### January 2027
- Membership launches alongside Signature.
- First members onboarded from the recurring-donor list with a "founding member" framing.
- First annual member gathering scheduled for later in Q1.

## Attorney flag

Minimal unless the perks package gets expensive. The IRS treats nominal-value membership perks (stickers, early access, a branded cup) as de minimis and not reducing the tax-deductibility of dues. If the program scales to include higher-value perks (free event tickets, private dinners with authors, catered receptions), dues may need to be split into deductible and non-deductible portions per IRS Publication 526. Flag on the attorney's list in fall 2026 as part of the design review.
