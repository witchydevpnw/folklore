# Shelf Life — Outreach Copy Pack

Working drafts for the three audiences Shelf Life depends on: authors, independent bookstores, and Little Free Library stewards. Each piece is written to be used as-is or edited before sending. Placeholders in `[brackets]` are context-specific details to fill in at send time.

## Suggested sequencing

Bookstore outreach should go first, in parallel or slightly ahead of the rest. Their consent affects what you can name publicly on the website and in social copy.

The author call and steward call can run in parallel once the bookstore piece is settled. The author list is evergreen and keeps getting built quarter over quarter. The steward roster is Q1-specific and closes once five are confirmed.

---

## 1. For Authors

**Purpose:** build a running list of eligible books for future Shelf Life shortlists. The ask is standing and stays live once the program is public.

### Instagram / Facebook feed post

> Writers of the Pacific Northwest: we want to know about your book.
>
> Shelf Life is a new quarterly program from Folklore Society. Every three months, readers vote on a shortlist of books by local and regional authors. The winner gets purchased from a Tri-Cities indie bookstore and tucked into Little Free Libraries across the metro with a small Folklore bookplate inside.
>
> We're building shortlists now. If you're a writer based in Washington, Oregon, or Idaho with a book out in the last few years, we want to hear about it. Any publication path works, from big-press to self-pub. Tri-Cities and Eastern Washington writers, you're the top of our list.
>
> DM us or email hello@folkloresocietywa.org with:
>
> - Your name
> - Book title
> - Publisher (or "self" if self-published)
> - Publication year
> - A link to buy it
>
> We'll reach out when your book lands on a shortlist.

### Instagram story version (shorter)

> Writers of the PNW:
>
> We're building the first Shelf Life shortlist. Shelf Life is our new quarterly program. Readers vote on a book by a local or regional author. We buy the winner from a Tri-Cities indie bookstore and circulate copies through Little Free Libraries.
>
> If you have a book out and you're in WA, OR, or ID, tell us about it.
>
> DM us or email hello@folkloresocietywa.org.

### Follow-up reply (when an author responds with their book)

> Thanks for sending your book our way. We're keeping a running list of eligible titles and pulling from it each quarter to build the shortlist of 3-4 books readers vote on. If your book lands on a shortlist, we'll be in touch before it goes public so you know it's coming.
>
> In the meantime, if you have a local event or reading coming up, send it our way. Happy to boost it to our community.

## 2. For Independent Bookstores

**Purpose:** get consent to name them in program materials, learn what they specialize in, and open an ongoing working relationship.

### Email — first outreach

**Subject:** Quick intro: Folklore Society and a new program called Shelf Life

> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm Candace from Folklore Society, a new literary nonprofit based in the Tri-Cities. We fundraise to bring authors out east, champion our independent bookstores, and support the readers and organizers already building a literary scene here.
>
> I'm reaching out because we're launching a quarterly program called Shelf Life, and your store would be part of the ecosystem it depends on.
>
> Each quarter, we publish a shortlist of 3-4 books by local and regional authors. Readers vote on their favorite. Whichever book wins, we buy copies from whichever Tri-Cities indie bookstore actually carries it, and we place those copies in Little Free Libraries across the metro. Every copy has a Folklore bookplate inside.
>
> Because different bookstores carry different genres, you wouldn't be the only source. The winning book determines where the copies come from. Romance wins? Copies come from BAGG. Literary fiction or nonfiction wins? Could come from you, depending on what you stock.
>
> A few things I wanted to ask:
>
> 1. Would you be comfortable being named in our program materials (website and eventual quarterly reports) as one of the Tri-Cities indie bookstores we purchase from?
> 2. What genres do you specialize in, and what's your usual stock profile for newer releases?
> 3. If a Shelf Life winner is a book you carry, what's the best way for us to order 5-20 copies at a time?
> 4. Anything else about Shelf Life that might be interesting to your store, or ways you'd want to be involved?
>
> Happy to grab coffee or jump on a call. Email me at hello@folkloresocietywa.org and we'll set a time.
>
> Thanks for the work you do. The Tri-Cities literary community runs on indie stores like yours.
>
> Warmly,
> Candace Delany
> President, Folklore Society
> folkloresocietywa.org

## 3. For Little Free Library Stewards

**Purpose:** recruit five stewards for the Q1 pilot. Prioritize responses that let you cover geographic spread across the metro.

### Instagram / Facebook feed post — open call

> Calling Little Free Library stewards of the Tri-Cities.
>
> We're starting a new quarterly program called Shelf Life. Every three months, readers vote on a book by a local or regional author. We buy copies from a Tri-Cities indie bookstore and circulate them through Little Free Libraries, each with a small Folklore bookplate inside saying where it came from.
>
> For our first quarter, we're looking for five stewards to join the pilot.
>
> If your LFL is somewhere in the Tri-Cities and you'd be up for hosting one Shelf Life book a quarter, DM us or email hello@folkloresocietywa.org with:
>
> - Your LFL's location (charter number if you have it)
> - Any logistical notes we should know (size, seasonal closures, access)
>
> Priority goes to a spread across Richland, West Richland, Kennewick, Pasco, and South Richland so every part of the metro has a Shelf Life book on day one. If you're in a less-covered area, raise your hand.

### Follow-up email (for interested responders)

**Subject:** Thanks for raising your hand for Shelf Life

> Hi [Name],
>
> Thanks for volunteering your LFL. Here's how a Shelf Life quarter works and what it looks like for a steward.
>
> Mid-quarter, we publish a shortlist of 3-4 books by regional authors. A public vote runs for about two weeks. The winner gets bought from a Tri-Cities indie bookstore that carries it, and a copy arrives at your door with a Folklore bookplate tucked inside and a short note for you.
>
> What we'd ask of you is minimal: place the book in your LFL when it arrives, let us know when it leaves (a DM or text is plenty), and, if you're willing, send us a photo of the book on the shelf for our quarterly report.
>
> No cost to you, ever. No commitment beyond one quarter at a time. If you want to step out after Q1, no hard feelings. If you want to stay in, we'll keep you on the roster.
>
> For Q1, we're finalizing the pilot group in [target timeframe]. If you're in, reply with your LFL's street address and we'll add you. Any questions, ask away.
>
> Warmly,
> Candace
