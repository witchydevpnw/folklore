What we do✦

01

Bringing writers to readers.

We fundraise to bring authors out to the Tri-Cities for readings, signings, and conversations. Some will be debut novelists, some will be names you recognize, some will be writers who live an hour down the road and deserve a proper room. Your donations make the guest list.

02

Championing local.

Independent bookstores, regional writers, and the small presses east of the Cascades. We buy from them, promote them, and make sure they're on every stage we help build.

03

Backing the people already doing the work.

The Tri-Cities already has book clubs, reading series, and people building literary community. What's missing is the fundraising and nonprofit structure to help it all grow. That's where we come in. We underwrite, we partner, we show up where we're useful.

About us✦

The Tri-Cities has readers, writers, bookstores, and people who care about all of it. It didn't have a nonprofit set up to support them. Now it does.

We fundraise. We underwrite author visits. We buy books from local stores for every event we run. We partner with the people who are already out here doing the work, and we make sure they have what they need to keep doing it.

Our board is made up of people who live this. A bookstore owner. An event producer. Readers who show up. We're Washington-incorporated, pursuing 501(c)(3) status, and every dollar that comes through Folklore Society goes right back into the literary community it came from.

We also operate as a licensed pop-up bar at a small number of literary events each year. That work underwrites some of what we do, and it's one of the ways we show up for partners whose events we believe in. But it isn't what we are. What we are is a nonprofit that exists so the Tri-Cities can have the reading life it deserves.
